About Memory Trail

Memory Trail is built to be easy to understand but hard to master. It’s a calm, skill‑based game where the only limit is how long you can keep the glowing trail in your mind.

Core idea

Follow the glowing path

  • Tiles on the grid light up one by one to show you the path.
  • Your goal is to remember the exact route.
  • Draw the same path with your finger, step by step.
  • Each round adds one more step to the trail.
Game feel

Designed for focus and relaxation

  • Clean, atmospheric forest‑themed visuals.
  • Smooth tile animations for a calm experience.
  • No timers rushing you – the only challenge is your own memory.
  • Perfect for short breaks, daily brain training or winding down.
Progress

Chasing your own best scores

  • Each correct path advances you to the next round.
  • One mistake ends the game – your score is the round you reached.
  • See how far you can go and beat your previous records.

How to play

Memory Trail is easy to learn in a few seconds. Here’s how the game works and what makes it progressively more challenging.

Step‑by‑step instructions

  • Press Play to start a new game.
  • Watch carefully as tiles light up one by one – this is the path you must memorize.
  • When it’s your turn, touch the starting tile and slide your finger to neighbouring tiles (up, down, left or right) to trace the same route.
  • If you follow the path correctly to the end, you advance to the next round and the path gets longer.
  • One mistake ends the game and your score is the round you reached.

Three difficulty levels

Choose how big a challenge you want. You can switch the grid size at any time and try to set a new record on each level:

  • 2×2 – four large tiles, short paths, great for a quick warm‑up.
  • 3×3 – classic grid with balanced difficulty.
  • 4×4 – more tiles, longer routes, serious memory training.

Highscores

For every grid size, the game keeps your top three scores:

  • best round reached
  • date and time when you achieved it

All scores are stored locally on your device. Check the High Score screen to see how your memory has improved over time.

Sound & haptics

Memory Trail uses subtle audio and vibration feedback to make the path feel more alive:

  • Optional sound effects for each tile and action.
  • Short vibrations on each correct step.
  • A small reward buzz on success and a longer buzz on game over.

You can toggle sounds and haptics on or off with a single button in the game.

Support & contact

If you run into issues, have questions or want to suggest new features, feel free to contact the developer.

Is the game free?

Yes, Memory Trail can be downloaded for free from the Google Play Store. There may be ads in the free version. A one‑time in‑app purchase can be used to remove ads.

Do I need an internet connection?

You can play Memory Trail without a constant internet connection. You will need internet to download the game and receive updates from the Play Store, and possibly to load ads.

Which devices are supported?

Memory Trail is designed for Android phones and tablets running Android 7.0 or newer. On the Play Store, you can see compatibility information for your own devices when you are logged in.
